Choosing the right hemp supplier shapes everything downstream: product quality,
compliance, and margin. Here is the framework we use.

## 1. Verify third-party testing

Every reputable supplier provides a current Certificate of Analysis (COA) from an
accredited lab. Check for cannabinoid potency, pesticides, heavy metals, and residual
solvents.

## 2. Confirm compliant sourcing

Ask where the hemp is grown and under which program. Domestic, licensed cultivation
with documented chain of custody reduces regulatory risk.

## 3. Pressure-test consistency

A great sample means little if batch-to-batch quality drifts. Request COAs across
multiple lots and compare.

> The cheapest supplier is rarely the cheapest once you price in failed batches and
> compliance headaches.
